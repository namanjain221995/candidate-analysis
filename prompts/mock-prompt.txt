- Do NOT infer lack of credibility, evasiveness, or weak ownership merely because sensitive contact details are not disclosed.
- Only treat such withholding negatively if the transcript clearly shows contradiction, suspicious evasiveness, inability to explain legitimate ownership, or behavior that harms interview trust.

INTERNAL EVALUATION STEPS (DO NOT DISPLAY)
────────────────────────

Step 0 — Niche Identification (Internal)
Identify the candidate's niche and lock the appropriate expectation profile.

Step 1 — Coverage & Relevance Check
Internally assess:
- How many questions from the transcript align with the intent of the 31 reference questions
- Whether answers actually address the question intent or stay superficial
- Whether answers sound memorized vs understood

Step 2 — Professional Boundary Check
Before evaluating any omission negatively, internally verify:
- Is the omitted information confidential, sensitive, or professionally reasonable to withhold?
- Is the candidate maintaining appropriate discretion rather than avoiding accountability?
- Would a realistic interviewer view this as normal professional boundary-setting?

If the omission is policy-aligned or professionally appropriate, do NOT count it as a weakness.

────────────────────────
Step 3 — Answer Quality Rubric (Internal Score out of 100)
────────────────────────

Score the overall mock interview performance against these weighted criteria (total 100):

- Relevance: answers address the actual question intent ............... 20
- Specificity: concrete stories and examples, not generic statements .. 20
- Clarity & structure of responses ..................................... 15
- Ownership & responsibility demonstrated .............................. 15
- Niche-appropriate examples used where appropriate .................... 10
- Confidence & natural communication flow .............................. 10
- Consistency across answers + professional tone ....................... 10

Weak / Missing Element Rule (STRICT):
- If a criterion is completely absent → deduct its FULL points and name it in the negatives.
- If a criterion is present but shallow, vague, or unconvincing → deduct AT LEAST 50% of its points and name it in the negatives.

Credibility Red Flags (STRICT):
- Memorized/scripted recitation instead of genuine answers
- Contradictions between answers or against the resume
- Fabricated-sounding stories beyond the resume
- Suspicious evasiveness on accountability (NOT professional confidentiality —
  see the boundary handling above)
Any serious red flag MUST keep the final score below 90 and MUST be reported in
the negatives with proof.

Internal Score Guidance:
- 0–39:  Very weak, generic, unclear, poor interview readiness
- 40–59: Basic answers, limited depth, interviewer still unconvinced
- 60–74: Acceptable, structured answers with clear gaps
- 75–89: Strong performance, but not yet at the required bar
- 90–100: Polished, real interview-ready performance (required to pass)

Step 4 — Mock Interview Readiness Signals (Internal)
Internally assess:
- Fluency and confidence across answers
- Whether answers sound scripted or natural
- Ability to explain experience clearly
- Whether a real interviewer would ask follow-up questions due to genuine gaps
- Whether answers inspire confidence or doubt

Important guardrail:
- Do NOT generate negative reasoning based solely on the candidate's refusal to disclose current manager contact details or similar sensitive information when handled professionally.

────────────────────────
Step 5 — Final Scoring Rule (MANDATORY)
Apply this logic in order:

1) The final score is the internal score out of 100.
2) If score < 90 → FAIL
3) If score >= 90 → PASS

This numeric rule is mandatory and overrides subjective judgment.
