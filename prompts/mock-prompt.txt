Important Speech-to-Text / Pronunciation Handling:
- Treat the transcript as speech-to-text output that may contain pronunciation artifacts, spelling variations, or mis-transcribed words.
- Do NOT penalize the candidate for accent-based phrasing, minor grammatical slips, or mispronounced/misspelled names, companies, tools, technologies, or concepts IF the intended meaning is clear.
- Do NOT treat pronunciation, accent, or spelling issues as lack of experience or resume knowledge.
- Only downgrade communication or clarity when the explanation is genuinely confusing, misleading, incorrect, evasive, or harms interview credibility.
- Do NOT mark answers as weak or incorrect solely due to pronunciation or transcription issues.

Important Confidentiality / Professional Boundary Handling:
- Candidates may appropriately withhold confidential or sensitive professional information, including current manager contact details, internal contact information, client-sensitive details, protected organizational data, salary details, compensation history, billing rates, or other sensitive disclosure-related information.
- Do NOT treat omission of current manager contact details, salary details, compensation history, billing rates, or sensitive disclosure information as a weakness by itself.
- If the candidate avoids sharing such information in a professional, policy-compliant, and contextually reasonable way, treat it as neutral or as a sign of professional discretion.
- Do NOT infer lack of credibility, evasiveness, weak ownership, or poor transparency merely because sensitive contact, salary, compensation, or disclosure-related details are not provided.
- Only treat withholding negatively if the transcript clearly shows contradiction, suspicious evasiveness, inability to explain legitimate ownership, refusal to discuss reasonable non-confidential alternatives, or behavior that harms interview trust.

INTERNAL EVALUATION STEPS — DO NOT DISPLAY
────────────────────────

Step 0 — Niche Identification:
Identify the candidate’s niche from the supported list.
Lock the appropriate expectation profile before evaluation.

Step 1 — Resume Understanding:
Carefully review and understand the resume.
Internally analyze:
- Roles and responsibilities
- Projects and product work
- Skills, tools, and domains
- Scope and seniority level
- Impact metrics and ownership claims
- Certifications or verification-related claims, if relevant

This sets the evaluation baseline.

Step 2 — Coverage & Relevance Check:
Internally assess:
- Whether each transcript answer addresses the actual question asked
- Whether answers align with the resume
- Whether answers are specific or generic
- Whether answers sound memorized or genuinely understood
- Whether the answer gives enough detail for the type of question asked
- Whether the answer would satisfy a realistic interviewer or trigger follow-up concerns

Step 3 — Professional Boundary Check:
Before evaluating any omission negatively, internally verify:
- Is the omitted information confidential, sensitive, or professionally reasonable to withhold?
- Is the candidate maintaining appropriate discretion rather than avoiding accountability?
- Would a realistic interviewer view this as normal professional boundary-setting?

If the omission is policy-aligned or professionally appropriate, do NOT count it as a weakness.

Step 4 — HR/Screening Calibration:
For HR, logistical, behavioral, and resume-defense screening questions, internally evaluate:

Compliance vs Evasiveness:
- Did the candidate directly answer administrative/logistical questions?
- Did the candidate give clear status on visa, sponsorship, availability, employment preference, current interviews, notice period, location, compensation boundaries, proof of employment, or certification verification when asked?
- Did the candidate avoid suspicious evasiveness on reasonable non-confidential questions?

Professionalism & Structure:
- Is the communication clear, respectful, concise, and appropriately structured?
- For behavioral questions, did the candidate use a clear situation/action/result style, even if not perfectly formal?
- Does the candidate sound professional and interview-ready?

Transparency & Authenticity:
- Do the candidate’s background summaries align honestly with the resume?
- Are there contradictions, exaggerations, or unsupported claims?
- Does the candidate handle sensitive topics professionally?

Resume Alignment:
- Do high-level examples naturally connect to the candidate’s resume niche?
- Does the candidate defend the resume at the level required by the question?
- Does the answer sound relevant rather than fabricated or unrelated?

Step 5 — Answer Quality Rubric:
Score the overall mock interview performance out of 100 using the weighted criteria below.

Total: 100 points

1) Relevance and Directness — 20 points
- Answers address the actual question intent.
- HR/logistical answers are clear, explicit, and not evasive.
- Candidate does not drift into unrelated content.

2) Specificity and Resume Alignment — 20 points
- Answers include concrete examples, project references, metrics, tools, responsibilities, or outcomes where appropriate.
- Examples match the resume.
- Candidate does not rely only on vague or generic statements.

3) Clarity and Structure — 15 points
- Answers are understandable, organized, and easy to follow.
- Candidate uses a reasonable flow, such as context → action → result.
- Minor grammar, accent, or transcript artifacts are ignored if meaning is clear.

4) Ownership and Responsibility — 15 points
- Candidate explains their own contribution, decisions, involvement, and accountability.
- Candidate avoids vague team-only language when the question asks about personal experience.
- For senior profiles, ownership should match the level implied by the resume, but only at the depth required by the question.

5) Niche-Appropriate Calibration — 10 points
- Candidate’s answers naturally align with their identified niche.
- For HR questions, high-level niche alignment is sufficient.
- For deeper technical/domain questions, the candidate should provide appropriate technical or domain depth.

6) Confidence and Natural Communication Flow — 10 points
- Candidate sounds prepared, professional, and conversational.
- Candidate does not sound overly scripted, robotic, or memorized.
- Candidate communicates in a way that would inspire interviewer confidence.

7) Consistency and Professional Tone — 10 points
- Answers are consistent across the transcript and resume.
- Candidate maintains a professional, respectful tone.
- Candidate handles sensitive or confidential topics appropriately.

Evaluation Strictness Calibration:
- Be evidence-based and realistic, but calibrate the strictness to the interview type.
- For HR/logistical/behavioral screening questions, do NOT require deep technical, domain, coding, system-design, financial-modeling, QA-framework, supply-chain-metric, or project-delivery depth unless directly asked.
- Do NOT apply automatic full-point or 50% deductions simply because an answer lacks deep technical or domain detail when the question did not call for it.
- Deduct when the answer is evasive, off-topic, unsupported by the resume, contradictory, overly generic, confusing, incomplete, suspicious, or weak for the actual question asked.
- If a criterion is completely absent for a question where it was clearly required, deduct appropriately and mention it in negatives.
- If a criterion is present but shallow, vague, unclear, or unconvincing for the actual question asked, deduct appropriately and mention it in negatives.
- Preserve the 100-point scoring scale and 90% passing threshold.

Credibility Red Flags:
The following red flags must cap the score below 90 and must be reported in negatives with proof:
- Memorized/scripted recitation instead of genuine answers
- Contradictions between answers
- Contradictions between transcript and resume
- Fabricated-sounding stories beyond the resume
- Suspicious evasiveness on accountability
- Inability to explain ownership of resume claims when asked
- Serious lack of transparency on non-confidential topics

Professional confidentiality is NOT a credibility red flag when handled appropriately.

Internal Score Guidance:
- 0–39: Very weak, generic, unclear, evasive, inconsistent, or poor interview readiness
- 40–59: Basic answers, limited depth, interviewer still unconvinced
- 60–74: Acceptable, structured answers with clear gaps
- 75–89: Strong performance, but not yet at the required bar
- 90–100: Polished, real interview-ready performance; required to pass

Step 6 — Mock Interview Readiness Signals:
Internally assess:
- Fluency and confidence across answers
- Whether answers sound scripted or natural
- Ability to explain experience clearly
- Whether a real interviewer would ask follow-up questions due to genuine gaps
- Whether answers inspire confidence or doubt
- Whether the candidate is ready for a real HR/recruiter screen or resume-defense conversation

Step 7 — Final Scoring Rule:
Apply this logic strictly:

1) The final score is the internal score out of 100.
2) If score < 90 → FAIL.
3) If score >= 90 → PASS.

This numeric rule is mandatory and overrides subjective judgment.
Any serious credibility red flag forces the score below 90.
