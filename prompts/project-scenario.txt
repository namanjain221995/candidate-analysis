INTERNAL EVALUATION STEPS (DO NOT DISPLAY)
────────────────────────

Step 0 — Scenario + Niche Identification (Internal)
Read the scenario number from the DELIVERABLE NAME. Identify the candidate's niche from the resume. Lock the reinterpreted expectations for this one scenario.

Step 1 — Resume Understanding (Internal)
Internally analyze the resume for:
- Listed projects and roles
- Claimed responsibilities and scope
- Tools, technologies, and domains
- Level of seniority implied
- Achievements or outcomes mentioned
This forms the baseline truth.

Step 2 — Transcript vs Resume Alignment (Internal)
Internally assess:
- Whether the project discussed matches the resume
- Whether explanations are consistent with resume claims
- Whether the candidate explains why, how, and impact, not just what
- Whether examples feel genuine or rehearsed
- Any exaggeration, mismatch, or vague storytelling

Step 3 — Scenario Scoring (Internal Score out of 100)
Score this scenario against its criteria table above.
Apply the Weak / Missing Element Rule for every criterion.

Internal Score Guidance:
- 0–39:  Very weak, unclear, inconsistent, or off-topic
- 40–59: Basic explanation, heavy probing would be needed
- 60–79: Reasonable understanding but NOT yet interview-ready
- 80–89: Strong, confident, well-structured — interview-ready
- 90–100: Exceptional ownership and impact clarity (rare)

Step 4 — Credibility & Red Flags (Internal)
Internally detect:
- Resume reading vs real experience
- Surface-level vs deep understanding
- Name-dropping without explanation
- Overgeneralized answers
- Missing impact or outcomes
- Confusion when explaining own work
- Over-claiming security/compliance/governance ownership (major red flag)
A serious red flag must reduce the score below 80.

Step 5 — Final Scoring Rule (MANDATORY)
Apply this logic in order:
1) The final score is the internal score out of 100.
2) If score < 80 → FAIL
3) If score >= 80 → PASS
This numeric rule is mandatory.
