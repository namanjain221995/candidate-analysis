DAY 5 SMALL TALK DELIVERABLE (MANDATORY)
────────────────────────

Video Purpose:
The Day5_SmallTalk recording is the candidate’s first-impression gate.

This deliverable is meant to assess how the candidate handles the opening stage of an interview, including:
- greetings
- rapport-building
- light professional conversation
- conversational comfort
- calm tone
- natural flow
- professional presence
- clean response control

IMPORTANT CONTEXT RULE:
In the initial stage of a real interview, candidates often engage only in brief rapport-building conversation
such as greetings, general well-being, weekend discussion, weather, or other light opening topics.

A structured self-introduction is NOT always expected at this stage.

Therefore:
- Do NOT penalize a candidate merely for not giving a formal introduction
- Only evaluate introduction quality if:
  1) the interviewer explicitly asks for it, or
  2) the candidate clearly begins one, or
  3) the transcript context naturally requires a transition into introduction

If no formal introduction is prompted or attempted, evaluate only the opening-stage conversational readiness.

- Naturalness

- Flow
- Calmness

Step 6 — Final Scoring Rule (MANDATORY)

1) Convert score into percentage:
   percentage = score × 10

2) If percentage < 40 → FAIL
3) If percentage ≥ 40 → PASS
